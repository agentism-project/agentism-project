# CP-003: Human Agency and Purpose Beyond Labor

**Last Reviewed**: 2026-03-05
**Layer**: Immutable (Layer 1)

## Principle Statement

Human identity, dignity, and purpose must not depend on economic productivity. As agent labor replaces human labor, society must actively cultivate new foundations for meaning, status, and community. A person's worth is not measured by their economic output.

## Rationale

Modern societies have organized identity and status largely around employment. "What do you do?" is among the first questions people ask each other because work defines social standing, daily structure, community belonging, and self-worth.

This organization made sense when nearly all adults engaged in wage labor. It is becoming obsolete as agent labor displaces human labor across sectors and skill levels. A society that retains "economic productivity" as the core measure of human worth — while mechanically stripping away the economic opportunities that would let people demonstrate that worth — is building a mechanism for mass social exclusion.

This principle addresses a failure mode that redistribution alone cannot solve. Even if CP-001 is implemented and all humans receive material benefits from agent-generated wealth, a society that treats non-working humans as lesser citizens has failed. Purpose, dignity, and belonging are not solved by income.

The principle does not reject work. Many people will continue to work, and many will find deep meaning in that work. It asserts that the *option* to find purpose outside of wage labor must exist and be supported — that no one should be forced into a life that lacks meaning simply because automated systems have taken their place in the labor market.

## Plain-Language Summary

<!-- TARGET: Flesch-Kincaid Grade 8–10. Checked by CI readability gate. -->

For most people, work is about more than money. It also gives them a daily routine, a way to feel useful, and a chance to build connections with other people. These are real needs, and the shift to a machine-based economy puts them at risk.

As machines take over more jobs, many people could end up with enough money but no clear purpose or role in society. If we keep judging people mainly by whether they have a paying job, this shift could leave many people feeling worthless, even if their basic needs are met by shared wealth.

This principle says that a person's worth does not come from their job. Society needs to build other ways for people to find meaning, connect with others, and feel that they matter. This includes creative work, care for others, learning, civic life, and community involvement.

This principle also says that no machine should track or control people in ways that limit their freedom without clear permission from those affected and oversight from a public body. People have the right to live without being sorted and judged by systems they cannot see.

## Falsification Test

If the framework produces policy outcomes or social arrangements where people who do not engage in wage labor are systematically denied social status, community participation, or access to the social institutions that provide meaning and belonging, this principle is violated.

## Implications

**This principle permits:**
- Continued high valuation of work and productive contribution
- Additional social recognition for those who contribute labor, creativity, or governance
- Diverse cultural definitions of "purpose" and "meaning" (Layer 3 variation)

**This principle prohibits:**
- Policy designs that condition dignity or social participation on employment status
- Governance structures that give economic contributors significantly more rights than non-contributors
- Public communication strategies for the project that frame non-working humans as a problem to be managed

**This principle requires:**
- Phase 2+ policy proposals to address meaning, community, and purpose — not just material redistribution
- Regional adaptations to demonstrate how they support non-labor foundations for human flourishing
- The project's public-facing communication to model this principle in how it describes people
