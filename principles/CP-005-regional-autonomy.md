# CP-005: Regional Autonomy Within Universal Rights

**Last Reviewed**: 2026-03-05

## Principle Statement

Each community, region, and nation has the right to adapt the Agentism framework to its own context, provided adaptation does not violate Principles CP-001 through CP-004.

## Rationale

A framework designed only for wealthy, industrialized democracies with high institutional trust is not a universal framework — it is a local solution dressed in universal language. Meaningful universality requires the framework to be adaptable to radically different political, economic, cultural, and institutional contexts.

The agent transition will arrive differently in different places. A West African smallholder economy navigating agricultural automation faces different challenges than a Northern European welfare state managing white-collar displacement. A country with functioning democratic institutions can rely on different deliberation mechanisms than one under authoritarian governance. A community with strong extended-family social structures may find non-labor foundations for purpose in ways a highly individualistic society cannot.

This principle acknowledges that diversity is not a problem to be resolved — it is a design requirement. The framework must be capable of generating meaningfully different regional implementations while maintaining the core commitments that make it coherent.

The constraint is equally important: regional adaptation cannot be a mechanism for escaping Principles I–IV. "Our culture doesn't value transparency" does not constitute a valid adaptation of CP-004. "Our tradition is that some people belong outside the economy" does not constitute a valid adaptation of CP-001. The boundary between legitimate cultural adaptation and principle violation must be actively maintained.

## Plain-Language Summary

<!-- TARGET: Flesch-Kincaid Grade 8–10. Checked by CI readability gate. -->

The world is big. There is no single solution that works for every country and community. A system that works in Sweden will not work in the same way in Nigeria or Indonesia.

This principle says each place has the right to find its own way. A community can design its own tools, its own processes, and its own policies — as long as they respect the four principles above.

Some things are not allowed as "adaptations." A region cannot say "we do not believe in transparency" to avoid Principle 4. A region cannot say "our culture excludes this group" to get around Principle 1. Those are not adaptations — they are violations.

But within those limits, the space is wide. Different cultures value different things. Different economies face different challenges. Local solutions are often better than global ones because local people understand local problems best.

Agentism does not tell you exactly what to do. It tells you what you cannot do — and leaves the rest to you.

## Falsification Test

If the framework forces a specific policy implementation on a region that contradicts demonstrated local needs, and that local implementation does not violate CP-001 through CP-004, this principle is violated. Conversely, if a region uses "cultural adaptation" as justification for denying universal entitlement (CP-001), operating without meaningful transparency (CP-004), or treating certain humans as outside the scope of protection, that adaptation itself violates this principle.

## Implications

**This principle permits:**
- Substantially different redistribution mechanisms across regions (Layer 3 variation)
- Different governance structures adapted to local institutional capacity
- Cultural variation in how purpose, community, and meaning are organized

**This principle prohibits:**
- Using "regional autonomy" as justification for principle violations
- Any global/central entity forcing a specific implementation on unwilling communities
- Treating regional diversity as evidence that universal principles do not apply

**This principle requires:**
- Regional adaptation proposals to include an explicit compliance check against CP-001 through CP-004
- A defined process for challenging a regional adaptation that appears to violate universal principles
- The `regional/` directory to maintain adaptation templates with built-in compliance guidance
