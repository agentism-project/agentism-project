# CP-001: Universal Entitlement to Agent-Generated Prosperity

## Principle Statement

Every human being has an inherent right to benefit from the prosperity generated by autonomous agents. When machines produce wealth, that wealth belongs — in part — to all of humanity, not exclusively to the owners of those machines.

## Rationale

Throughout history, ownership of productive resources has determined who benefits from their output. Land, factories, and capital have historically concentrated wealth among those who owned them. Autonomous agents — AI systems, robots, and algorithms — represent the next generation of productive resources, and they are developing at a pace that could produce the most significant concentration of productive capacity in human history.

If the benefits of agent labor flow only to the owners of those agents, the result is a world where the majority of humanity becomes economically irrelevant through no fault of their own. This is not a market failure to be corrected — it is the predicted outcome of unmodified market dynamics when applied to automated production.

This principle asserts that the right to benefit is inherent — it does not depend on economic contribution, nationality, employment status, or any other condition. The *how* of redistribution (taxes, dividends, public services, compute access) is deliberately left to adaptive processes. The *that* of redistribution is non-negotiable.

## Plain-Language Summary

<!-- TARGET: Flesch-Kincaid Grade 8–10. Checked by CI readability gate. -->

Every person on Earth has the right to benefit from the wealth that machines create, and this right cannot be taken away by any law or policy. It does not depend on having a job, owning property, or living in a rich country.

Today, when a robot or an AI system does work that a person used to do, most of the profit goes to the company that owns that machine. This principle says that arrangement is not good enough on its own. The machines that create wealth today were built on top of knowledge and public order that all of society built together over many years.

This principle does not say everyone must get the same amount. It does not stop people from earning more through their own work and skills. What it says is that no one should be left out entirely just because they do not own the machines doing the work. The exact methods for sharing the wealth can vary across countries and change over time, but the right itself stays fixed.

## Falsification Test

If the framework produces a policy where a defined category of humans (defined by nationality, employment status, age, health, or any other characteristic) is systematically excluded from agent-generated benefits, this principle is violated.

## Implications

**This principle permits:**
- Variation in redistribution mechanisms across regions and phases
- Additional benefits for those who contribute labor, creativity, or governance
- Gradual phase-in of redistribution as agent deployment scales

**This principle prohibits:**
- Policies that categorically exclude any human population from baseline entitlement
- Frameworks that treat agent-generated wealth as purely private property with no social obligation
- Regional adaptations that deny the basic entitlement to any subgroup

**This principle requires:**
- All Layer 3 policy proposals to demonstrate how they ensure universal inclusion
- Impact assessments for any proposed redistribution mechanism
