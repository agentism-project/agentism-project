# Glossary

A living glossary of key terms used in the Agentism project.
Terms are added as they appear in project documents.

If a term is missing or poorly defined, open a Content Suggestion issue
or post in [GitHub Discussions](../README.md#discussions).

---

## A

**Agentism**  
A political and economic framework for the age of automated production. Agentism holds that when machines do most of the work, the wealth they create should be shared with everyone — and that this shift must be designed on purpose by society, not left to market forces alone.

**Autonomous agent**  
A software or robotic system that performs productive work without ongoing human instruction. Examples include AI writing tools, industrial robots, and trading algorithms. Autonomous agents range from simple scripts that automate a single task to complex systems capable of independent decision-making.

---

## D

**Deliberate transition**  
The idea that the shift to automated production must be planned by society — not left to chance or market forces alone. This choice should be made through open public debate, with all affected people having a real say. See
[CP-002](../principles/CP-002-deliberate-transition.md).

---

## R

**Regional autonomy**  
The right of communities and nations to adapt the Agentism framework to fit their local needs, as long as the five core principles are not broken. See [CP-005](../principles/CP-005-regional-autonomy.md).

---

## U

**Universal entitlement**  
The right of every person to receive some share of the wealth that machines create. How that share is divided can vary across places and change over time, but the basic right to some share cannot be taken away. See [CP-001](../principles/CP-001-universal-entitlement.md).
