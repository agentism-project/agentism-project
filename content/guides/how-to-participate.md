# How to Participate in the Agentism Project

**You do not need to be a developer to contribute.**

Agentism is a project about society, economics, and the future of work. We need economists, philosophers, educators, policy experts, translators, community organizers, and people from all walks of life. Technical expertise is one kind of contribution — not the only one.

---

## Option 1: GitHub Discussions

The easiest way to start. No technical knowledge required.

1. Go to the [Agentism GitHub repository](https://github.com/agentism-project/agentism-project)
2. Click the **Discussions** tab
3. Browse existing conversations or start a new one

You can ask questions, share ideas, give feedback on principle documents, and connect with other contributors. A GitHub account is free to create.

---

## Option 2: Email

If you prefer not to use GitHub, write to: **agentism.project@gmail.com**

We read all emails. If you have a formal proposal or a detailed suggestion, include "PROPOSAL:" at the start of the subject line and we will process it through the governance system.

---

## Option 3: Working Groups

Working groups are the core engines of the project. Each group owns a domain and produces papers, proposals, and policies.

Current working groups:

| Group | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| Economics | How wealth flows from agents to humans |
| Governance | Political structures and decision processes |
| Technology | AI infrastructure and compute access |
| Education & Meaning | Purpose beyond labor, care, resilience |
| Legal & Regulatory | Laws and rights for the agent economy |
| Communication | Plain language, outreach, media strategy |
| Ecology & Physical Limits | Environmental sustainability of AI |

To join any working group, email agentism.project@gmail.com with the subject line "JOIN WORKING GROUP: [group name] — [your name]".

---

## Option 4: Review and Feedback

Every principle document and governance proposal benefits from plain-language review. You do not need expertise in the topic — you need to be able to say "I did not understand this sentence" or "this example helped me."

Documents available for review are marked **DRAFT** in their header. Find them in the [`principles/`](../../principles/) and [`governance/`](../../governance/) directories.

Submit feedback by email or by opening a [Content Suggestion issue](../../.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/content-suggestion.md) on GitHub.

---

## Option 5: Translations

We need the project documents translated into many languages. Spanish, French, Portuguese, Arabic, Mandarin, Hindi, and Swahili are priorities.

If you can translate any document, email agentism.project@gmail.com. We will pair you with a native speaker reviewer from the Translation team.

---

## What Happens to Your Contribution

All contributions are reviewed before being integrated. You will receive a response within 30 days. If your contribution is accepted, you are credited by name (or pseudonym, if you prefer).

If your contribution is not accepted, you will receive written feedback explaining why, with guidance on how to revise and resubmit.

---

## What This Project Does Not Do

- We do not sell your data
- We do not charge for participation
- We do not promise money or rewards for contributions
- We do not ask for financial contributions during Phase 0 (this will change in Phase 1 — see the transparency framework)
