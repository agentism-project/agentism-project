**Last updated**: 2026-03-06

## Trademarks

"**Agentism**" and the Agentism symbol are trademarks of the Agentism Project. They identify this specific project and its official publications. The official symbol and brand assets are published in the [`brand/`](brand/) directory.

## Purpose of This Policy

The Agentism Project publishes its content under an open license (CC BY-SA 4.0) and its code under the MIT License. These licenses grant broad permissions to use, modify, and distribute the *content and code*.

However, open licensing does **not** grant permission to use the project's **name or symbol** in ways that could create confusion about what is or isn't the official Agentism Project. This policy clarifies the boundary.

## You MAY

- **Refer to the Agentism Project by name** in articles, academic papers, social media, discussions, and other commentary. This is standard nominative fair use.
- **Link to the project** and quote from its published materials (with attribution per CC BY-SA 4.0).
- **Create derivative works** from Agentism's published content under CC BY-SA 4.0, provided you follow the rules below.
- **State that your work is "based on" or "derived from" the Agentism Project**, provided the statement is accurate.

## You MAY NOT

- **Name your own project, product, organization, or service "Agentism"** or any confusingly similar variation (e.g., "Agentism AI", "The Agentism Foundation", "Agentism Pro").
- **Use the Agentism symbol** in a way that implies endorsement, affiliation, or official status that does not exist.
- **Represent derivative works as the official Agentism Project**. If you modify and redistribute Agentism content (permitted under CC BY-SA 4.0), you must clearly state:
  - That your version is a modification, not the original.
  - That it is not endorsed by or affiliated with the Agentism Project.
  - Where the original can be found.
- **Use the Agentism name or symbol in commercial products or services** without explicit written permission from the project maintainers.

## Derivative Works — Required Notice

If you create a derivative work under CC BY-SA 4.0, include this notice prominently:

> This work is derived from material published by the Agentism Project (https://github.com/agentism-project/agentism-project) and is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0. This is not the official Agentism Project and is not endorsed by or affiliated with it.

## Requesting Permission

For uses not covered above, contact: **agentism.project@gmail.com**

We will respond within 14 days. Permission is granted or denied at the sole discretion of the project maintainers.

## Enforcement

This policy is enforced through:
1. **Common-law trademark rights** established by first use in public activity (March 2026).
2. **Community governance** — the Agentism community monitors for unauthorized use.
3. **Formal registration** may be pursued if the project reaches sufficient scale and impact.

## Why This Matters

Open-source and open-content projects are vulnerable to name appropriation — someone takes the name, attaches it to something unrelated or harmful, and dilutes the original project's identity. This policy exists to protect the movement's integrity while keeping its ideas freely available.

The ideas are open. The name is protected.
