# Working Group: Legal and Regulatory

**Domain**: Legislative frameworks, rights architecture, enforcement mechanisms, and regional law

**Status**: FORMING (Phase 0 — charter published, members not yet recruited)
**Layer**: Adaptive (Layer 3 outputs), Durable (charter)

## Scope

The Legal and Regulatory Working Group owns the legal architecture of the Agentism framework. It answers: *What laws need to change, and how do you translate Agentism's principles into enforceable legal and regulatory frameworks?*

Primary questions:
- What legislative changes are necessary to enable agent-based redistribution?
- How do you define AI ownership in law — and what obligations come with it?
- How do you create accountability mechanisms for autonomous agent systems?
- What international legal frameworks are needed for a global transition?
- How do legal rights (privacy, autonomy, economic participation) need to expand in an agent economy?

## Out of Scope

- Economic mechanism design (→ Economics WG)
- Technical standards and interoperability (→ Technology WG)
- Political coordination between nations (→ Governance WG)

## Deliverables (Phase 1 Target)

- [ ] Working paper: "AI Ownership Law — Current State and Required Changes"
- [ ] Working paper: "Rights Architecture for the Agent Economy"
- [ ] Policy brief: "Legislative Priorities" — quick-win legal changes vs long-term structural reforms
