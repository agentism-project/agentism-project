# Working Group: Education and Meaning

**Domain**: Purpose beyond labor, emotional intelligence, interpersonal care, skill transitions, identity, community, and resilience

**Status**: FORMING (Phase 0 — charter published, members not yet recruited)
**Layer**: Adaptive (Layer 3 outputs), Durable (charter)

## Scope

The Education and Meaning Working Group owns the human dimension of the post-labor transition. It answers: *When machines do the cognitive work, what do humans do — and how do we build systems that support flourishing rather than collapse?*

Primary questions:
- What replaces work as the foundation of identity, purpose, and daily structure?
- How do we educate for a world where most cognitive tasks are automated but empathy, care, and human connection remain uniquely human?
- What skills become more valuable as automation increases?
- How do communities maintain social cohesion when traditional employment structures dissolve?
- What do we know from psychology about transitions in purpose and identity?

## Out of Scope

- Technical skills training for working with AI (→ Technology WG)
- Economic policies supporting non-labor lifestyles (→ Economics WG)

## Deliverables (Phase 1 Target)

- [ ] Working paper: "Meaning Beyond Work — Framework for Post-Labor Identity"
- [ ] Working paper: "Care Economy in the Agent Era" — value of emotional labor, interpersonal connection, and community maintenance
- [ ] Policy brief: "Education Reform Priorities" — what skills and experiences should education prioritize?
