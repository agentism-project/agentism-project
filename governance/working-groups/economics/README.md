# Working Group: Economics

**Domain**: Redistribution mechanisms, capital taxation, transition funding, economic modeling, and deflationary transition scenarios

**Status**: FORMING (Phase 0 — charter published, members not yet recruited)
**Layer**: Adaptive (Layer 3 outputs), Durable (charter)

## Scope

The Economics Working Group owns the economic design of the Agentism framework. It answers the foundational question: *How does wealth flow from agents to humans, equitably and sustainably?*

Primary questions:
- What replaces wage-based taxation when human labor is largely displaced?
- What redistribution mechanisms are viable across different political economies?
- What are the deflationary collapse scenarios (AI destroys jobs but fails to generate redistributable profits), and how does the framework respond?
- How does transition funding work — who pays for the transition, when, and through what mechanisms?

## Out of Scope

- Legal and legislative implementation (→ Legal & Regulatory WG)
- International coordination institutions (→ Governance WG)
- Environmental impact of AI infrastructure (→ Ecology WG)

## Deliverables (Phase 1 Target)

- [ ] Working paper: "Redistribution Mechanism Survey" — comparison of UBI, sovereign wealth fund dividends, data/compute taxation, and other models
- [ ] Working paper: "Deflationary Transition Scenarios" — what happens if AI generates no redistributable surplus?
- [ ] Policy brief: "Phase 0 Economic Design Constraints" — what the framework cannot do, and why
