# Working Group: Governance

**Domain**: Political structures, decision-making systems, and international coordination

**Status**: FORMING (Phase 0 — charter published, members not yet recruited)
**Layer**: Adaptive (Layer 3 outputs), Durable (charter)

## Scope

The Governance Working Group owns the political architecture of the Agentism framework. It answers: *What governance structures — local, national, and global — are needed to manage the agent transition?*

Primary questions:
- How do nations coordinate on AI development and benefit-sharing?
- What new institutions (international, supranational, or federated) are needed?
- How do existing governance systems need to adapt?
- What governance models work in contexts with weak or authoritarian state structures?

Note: This group's scope is *external* governance design — governance of society's relationship with AI agents. The Agentism project's *internal* governance is defined in [`decision-making.md`](../../decision-making.md) and [`roles.md`](../../roles.md).

## Out of Scope

- Internal project governance structure (defined in governance root documents)
- Legal/legislative drafting (→ Legal & Regulatory WG)

## Deliverables (Phase 1 Target)

- [ ] Working paper: "International AI Governance Gap Analysis" — what coordination mechanisms exist vs what is needed
- [ ] Working paper: "Governance in Weak-State Contexts" — how the framework applies outside stable democracies
- [ ] Policy brief: "Minimum Viable Governance" — what governance changes are prerequisites before meaningful redistribution can occur?
