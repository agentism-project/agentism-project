# Working Group: Technology

**Domain**: AI development standards, compute access, Digital Public Infrastructure, sovereign AI, and Universal Basic Compute

**Status**: FORMING (Phase 0 — charter published, members not yet recruited)
**Layer**: Adaptive (Layer 3 outputs), Durable (charter)

## Scope

The Technology Working Group owns the technical architecture of the Agentism framework. It answers: *How do we ensure every person has meaningful access to agent-generated productivity — and that AI power does not monopolize?*

Primary questions:
- How do we prevent AI monopolies forming around foundational model infrastructure?
- Should foundational AI be treated as Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) — a public utility?
- How does every person gain access to agent labor (Universal Basic Compute)?
- How do we ensure nations are not dependent on corporate AI monopolies for critical infrastructure (sovereign AI)?
- What technical standards enable interoperability and migration between AI platforms?

## Out of Scope

- Environmental sustainability of AI infrastructure (→ Ecology WG)
- Economic design of redistribution (→ Economics WG)
- Educational approaches to technology skills (→ Education & Meaning WG)

## Deliverables (Phase 1 Target)

- [ ] Working paper: "Digital Public Infrastructure for AI" — what public AI infrastructure should look like
- [ ] Working paper: "Universal Basic Compute" — design parameters for universal agent access
- [ ] Policy brief: "AI Monopoly Prevention Mechanisms" — technical and regulatory approaches
