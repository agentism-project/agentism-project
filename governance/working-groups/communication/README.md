# Working Group: Communication

**Domain**: Public messaging, plain language, anti-fear framing, accessibility, and media strategy

**Status**: FORMING (Phase 0 — charter published, members not yet recruited)
**Layer**: Adaptive (Layer 3 outputs), Durable (charter)

## Scope

The Communication Working Group owns the public-facing voice of the Agentism framework. It answers: *How do we explain this to a general audience — and how do we counter fearmongering and distortion?*

Primary questions:
- How do you explain a complex socio-economic framework in plain language?
- What communication strategies reduce fear about automation and job displacement?
- How do you communicate across radically different cultural and political contexts?
- What do anti-Agentism arguments look like, and how should we respond to them?
- How do we build a global community with diverse languages and communication styles?

This group is also responsible for ensuring all project documents meet readability requirements (FK Grade 8-10 for public-facing content) and for the glossary maintained in [`content/glossary.md`](../../../content/glossary.md).

## Out of Scope

- Content strategy for specific policy proposals (→ relevant domain WG)
- Platform architecture (→ Technology WG, infrastructure decisions)

## Deliverables (Phase 1 Target)

- [ ] Style guide: "Plain Language Guide for Agentism Contributors"
- [ ] Working paper: "Counter-Narrative Compendium" — documented critiques and responses
- [ ] Glossary expansion: 20 key terms with plain-language definitions
- [ ] One-page explainer for general audiences (multiple languages)
