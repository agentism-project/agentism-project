# Working Group: Ecology and Physical Limits

**Domain**: Environmental impact of AI infrastructure, energy consumption, raw material extraction, e-waste, and data center governance

**Status**: FORMING (Phase 0 — charter published, members not yet recruited)
**Layer**: Adaptive (Layer 3 outputs), Durable (charter)

## Scope

The Ecology Working Group owns the physical sustainability dimension of the Agentism framework. It answers: *What are the physical limits of scaling AI — and how do we prevent the agent economy from destroying the ecological foundations it depends on?*

Primary questions:
- What are the physical limits of scaling AI infrastructure (energy, water, raw materials)?
- How do we prevent "AI colonialism" — where developing nations' natural resources (cobalt, copper, energy, water) are extracted for offshore data centers with no local benefit?
- How do we ensure the environmental cost of agent-based production does not undermine the benefits promised by CP-001?
- What governance mechanisms can constrain AI's ecological footprint?
- How does the environmental cost of training and running AI systems factor into any redistribution accounting?

## Out of Scope

- General environmental policy beyond AI infrastructure
- Technical efficiency improvements (→ Technology WG)

## Deliverables (Phase 1 Target)

- [ ] Working paper: "AI Infrastructure Environmental Accounting" — a methodology for calculating ecological costs
- [ ] Working paper: "AI Colonialism — Resource Extraction and Developing Nations"
